About NestX

A smarter way to move home in New Zealand

What is NestX?

Moving home is one of the biggest decisions you'll ever make — and it doesn't have to mean handing over tens of thousands of dollars in agent fees. NestX is a private marketplace where New Zealand homeowners can list their property, connect with others looking to move, and explore whether a direct swap makes more sense than the traditional sale process.

A swap sale is simply two private sales happening at the same time. Both homeowners agree on their property values, the difference is settled between them, and a licensed NZ conveyancer takes care of the legal side. No agent in the middle. No commission. Just two people helping each other find a better fit.

NestX is a private listing platform only. We connect willing parties — we don't act as a real estate agent, provide valuations, or give advice on whether a deal is right for you. We always recommend getting independent legal and financial advice before entering any agreement.

How a swap sale works

It's simpler than you might think. Here's the journey from listing to keys in hand.

1

Create your account & verify your identity

Sign up and upload a government-issued photo ID. We review it manually and apply your verified badge within 1–2 business days. Optionally add a police check for extra trust.

2

List your home

Add your property details, photos, asking price and swap preference. Only your username shows publicly — your real name and contact details stay hidden.

3

Browse & send a swap offer

Find a home you love and send a swap offer — including your property details and the cash difference you're proposing. The other party reviews it at their own pace.

💬 Both parties click "Open to chat" — contact details are shared and the conversation begins

4

Agree on values

Talk directly, get independent valuations if needed, and agree on the cash difference. There's no obligation until formal agreements are signed — either party can walk away at this stage.

5

Engage a conveyancer

Both parties appoint a licensed NZ conveyancer — ideally one experienced in simultaneous settlements. They draft the sale and purchase agreements for both properties. Browse our directory for recommendations.

6

Settlement day 🎉

Both transfers complete simultaneously. The cash difference is settled. Keys are exchanged. You're in your new home — without paying a cent in commission.

Questions we get asked a lot

Is a home swap legal in New Zealand?

Absolutely. Private property sales are completely legal in NZ without a real estate agent. A swap is simply two simultaneous private sales — perfectly ordinary under NZ property law, just handled a little differently.

Do I need a real estate agent?

No — you don't need an agent to sell or swap privately in New Zealand. You do need a licensed conveyancer to handle the legal side, and we strongly recommend engaging one early. Our conveyancer directory is a great place to start.

What name appears on my listing?

Just your username — nothing more. Your real name, email address and phone number stay completely private until you choose to become open to chat with someone.

What does "Open to chat" mean?

When both parties click "Open to chat", it means you're interested in exploring things further. At that point, first names and contact details are shared so you can talk directly. It's not a commitment — either of you can still walk away. Nothing is binding until your conveyancer has formal agreements signed.

How is the cash difference worked out?

You and the other party agree on each home's value — ideally with independent registered valuations to give everyone confidence. The owner of the lower-value property pays the difference to the other as part of the settlement. Your conveyancer handles the mechanics.

What if we can't agree on values?

That's okay — it happens. You can both get independent registered valuations (typically $800–$1,200 in NZ) to get an objective view. If you still can't reach agreement, there's no obligation to proceed. No harm done.

What does NestX charge?

A one-off registration fee of 0.1% of your property's Rateable Value (RV), with a minimum of $500 NZD.

There's an optional Premium upgrade for $199 — this lets your listing accept both swap and straight purchase offers, and gives you the option to show your contact details publicly.

No commission on any swap or sale. Every dollar of your profit stays with you.

What if the other party backs out?

Until formal sale and purchase agreements are signed with your conveyancers, either party can withdraw without penalty. Once signed, standard NZ property law conditions apply — your conveyancer will explain any conditions or exit clauses.

Can I swap with someone in a different city?

Yes — though many swaps work well within the same region simply for practical reasons. Cross-city swaps absolutely happen, especially among people who are specifically looking to relocate.

What if I have a mortgage?

Your bank will need to be part of the settlement process. Most mortgages are portable or can be discharged and re-drawn on your new property. Your conveyancer handles this coordination — just make sure to speak to your bank early so there are no surprises.

Is NestX a real estate agent?

No. NestX is a private listing platform — an online space where willing parties can find each other. We're not licensed under the Real Estate Agents Act 2008 and don't carry out agency work. We don't negotiate deals or advise on property values. Always get independent advice from people qualified to give it.
